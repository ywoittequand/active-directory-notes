# Defensive Notes

## Overview

Preventing lateral movement requires reducing administrative exposure, limiting credential reuse, and protecting privileged accounts.

---

# Key Defensive Principles

## Least Privilege

Reduce unnecessary administrative access.

---

## Administrative Tiering

Separate privileged administration from standard user activity.

---

## Session Isolation

Prevent privileged accounts from authenticating to lower-trust systems.

---

## Password Management

Use:

* LAPS
* Unique passwords
* Strong password rotation

---

## Segmentation

Restrict communication paths between systems.

---

## Monitoring

Monitor for:

* Remote administration abuse
* Privileged session exposure
* Unusual authentication activity

---

# Strategic Focus

Organizations should continuously evaluate:

* Attack paths
* Administrative relationships
* Tier 0 exposure
* Privileged account usage
